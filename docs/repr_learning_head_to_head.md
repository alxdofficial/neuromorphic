# Memory architectures — head-to-head results

_Generated by `scripts/repr_learning/build_results_doc.py` from the saved per-sample files._

**Setup:** frozen Llama-3.2-1B decoder + a side-car memory (128 tokens × d_llama, ~49M params each, matched). 5 families × 48 questions = **240 samples per model**. babiLong is held-out OOD.


## Metrics we report

| metric | what it measures | punishes verbosity? | punishes paraphrase? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EM** | exact match after normalization | yes (strict) | yes |
| **Containment** | gold answer appears as a whole-token span in the output | **no** | yes |
| **Judge** | LLM judge (Gemini 3 Flash), 7-category rubric → score | **no** | **no** |

Judge is the headline (matches the goal: *did the model give the right answer, regardless of phrasing?*). EM/Containment are the strict, free, deterministic lexical floor. (EM ≤ Containment ≤ Judge by construction.)

## LLM-judge category breakdown (counts /240) — the per-case detail

Score mapping: correct=1.0, correct_verbose=1.0, incomplete=0.5, partially_wrong=0.25, wrong_value=0.0, irrelevant=0.0, no_answer=0.0 (re-weightable — categories are saved per sample).

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

**The real story:** across every model **~83% of answers are `wrong_value`** — the right *type* of answer (a year/name/place) with the wrong *value* = confident confabulation. Only ~10% are genuinely `correct`. These memories mostly *confabulate plausibly* rather than retrieve.
